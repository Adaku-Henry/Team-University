Common Questions

Academic FAQs

What are the minimum entry requirements?

Entry requirements vary by program level. Undergraduate programs require a minimum of 5 O-Level credits including English and Mathematics. Diploma programs require at least 3 O-Level credits. Graduate programs require a relevant first degree with a minimum classification of 2:2 (Second Class Lower). Specific faculty requirements may apply — contact the Admissions Office for full details.

Do you offer online and distance learning?

Yes. Benchmark Business College offers a robust blended learning model through our Online Learning Portal. Several programs are available in full-online and weekend modes to accommodate working professionals. Students access lecture materials, submit assignments, and interact with lecturers through our e-learning platform.

Are your programs accredited?

Yes. All our academic programs are fully accredited by the Zambia Qualifications Authority (ZAQA) and the Higher Education Authority (HEA). Our Health Sciences programs additionally hold professional registration with the Health Professions Council of Zambia (HPCZ). We are also affiliated with several international academic bodies.

What scholarship opportunities are available?

We offer merit-based scholarships, need-based bursaries, and partial fee waivers. Students with distinction-level results at entry are eligible for academic merit scholarships covering up to 30% of tuition fees. Corporate bursaries through partner organisations are also available. Contact the Student Finance Office for applications.

How do I register for a new semester?

Students register through the Student Information Portal (SIP) using their student ID and password. Registration opens 2 weeks before the semester begins and closes at the end of the first week of classes. Late registration attracts an additional fee. Ensure fees are cleared or a payment plan is agreed upon before registering.

Does Benchmark offer international student programs?

Yes. We welcome international students from across Africa and beyond. We assist with student visa applications, offer orientation programs, and have dedicated international student support services. Some programs offer English proficiency test waivers for SADC nationals. Contact our International Admissions desk for guidance.

What student accommodation is available?

On-campus student accommodation is available for both male and female students on a first-come, first-served basis. Rooms include Wi-Fi, study desks, and communal kitchen facilities. Off-campus accommodation assistance is available through our Student Housing Registry with a list of approved, affordable properties near campus.
